9:45am to 10:30am Introducing the Integrated Data Service

As part of Government Reform and its ambition to be more data driven, the ONS is leading in the delivery of an Integrated Data Service in collaboration with partners across government. Integrated Data Service (IDS) is for analysts in government, Devolved Administrations and external researchers to provide controlled access to a series of integrated Critical National Data Assets built, linked and maintained for deep analysis. In this session, Dominic Hale, the Head of Strategy for the Integrated Data Service will provide an overview of IDS, the achievements so far and the plans for the future.
This session is for government employees only.

Format: Presentation and discussion
Speakers:
Dominic Hale is the Head of Strategy for the Integrated Data Service. His role includes setting the strategic direction of the programme, while ensuring alignment to government priorities and transformation across the DDAT domain, including the National Data Strategy & Declaration on Government Reform. Dominic has a strong background in project delivery and data analysis, having spent the last decade within the digital and data arena and previous to that, as an analyst.
Ruth James is the Strategic Cross Government Lead for the Integrated Data Programme. Her role involves engagement across government to ensure the programme delivers on collaborative commitments and priorities. Ruth has worked in data, research and official statistics for many years and has held a variety of roles including design of data collection practices, assessment of official statistics, and more recently in data strategy and governance.

1:30pm to 3:00pm Managing Data Taxonomies across government

This session brings together speakers from Gatsby Foundation, Nesta, the Office for National Statistics and others to discuss challenges of using taxonomies across government, both statistical and operational. This will be a practical session with examples from Skills, Vulnerability, Industry and Classification of Individual Consumption by Purpose (COICOP). We’ll also introduce the concept of collaborative and centralised governance through a cross-government Taxonomy Oversight Group.

Format: Panel discussion

3:30pm to 4:15pm Defra Data Services Platform

A demonstration of the Defra Data Services Platform (DSP). This is an online suite of services that enables users to access data from Defra, the Environment Agency, and other organisations in the Defra group. We will look at the DSP’s data download functionality, and its APIs, apps, and customer forum, and we will explain how these can be accessed and used by different types of users
