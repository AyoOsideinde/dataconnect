12.00pm to 12.30pm Scottish Local Government Data Task Force

Hear about how Scotland’s Local Authorities have set up a joint Data Task Force to help them harness the power of data.

Local Authorities in Scotland have joined forces with the Digital Office for Scottish Local Government to understand how they can use data to achieve positive outcomes for their organisations, the wider sector, and citizens. The Data Task Force are also collaborating with the Scottish Government on a number of challenges from improving data maturity, the development of a Data Transformation Framework, and to make it easier to find public sector data through a CivTech Innovation Challenge.

Speakers:
- Kimberley Hose, Head of Business Intelligence Glasgow City Council and Chair of the Data Task Force Advisory Group
- Alex Hamilton, Business Development Officer for North Lanarkshire Council and Chair of the Data Task Force Capability Squad
- Shona Nicol, Data and Digital Identity Division - Scottish Government

3.00pm to 4.00pm - Strengthening the Quality of HMRC Statistics

We will be presenting a summary of actions the Office for Statistics Regulation (OSR) and HMRC have taken to improve the quality of HMRC’s statistics. HMRC has developed and started a plan of action to implement recommendations from a review by the OSR of the quality of HMRC’s statistics, published in 2020.

Format: Presentation with follow-up discussion.

Audience: The session is relevant to analysts who rely on robust, good quality data and statistics, for example statistics producers, statisticians, economists and data scientists.

Presenters:
Chris Davies, Office for Statistics Regulation - Chris joined the OSR in 2016, based in its Newport Office. Over the last twenty years he has worked with and been involved in the production of business and economic statistics in a variety of economist roles at the Office for National Statistics, the UK and European Intellectual Property Offices and in the Finance sector.
Jackie Orme, HMRC – Jackie has worked as a government statistician for nearly 30 years in several departments including HMRC, DCMS and ONS. In her current role she has led the design and coordination of work across HMRC’s analytical directorate to implement recommendations from the OSR’s review of HMRC’s statistics.
