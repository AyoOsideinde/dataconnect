DataConnect21 guide for speakers and session hosts

27 September to 1 October 2021. Hosted by the Data Standards Authority and the Government Data Quality Hub.

Download this speaker guide as a PDF

DataConnect21 is a week-long festival of all things data across government, local government and the UK public sector, running from 27 September to 1 October 2021.

It's co-hosted by the Data Standards Authority (part of the Central Digital and Data Office at the Cabinet Office) and the Government Data Quality Hub at the Office for National Statistics.

Anyone and everyone in the UK public sector interested in data will come together online to tell and hear stories about data in government and reflect on the wider effect that better use of data is having across the whole of government.

Tell your story at DataConnect21

You're invited to take part. You do not need to be a data expert. We’re interested in hearing from a diverse range of people doing interesting work with data.

DataConnect21 is a unique opportunity for you to connect with a wide audience to share your team’s data stories and successes.

If you're outside the UK public sector, we’re still keen to hear from you.

If you've built a service or carried out research using publicly available public sector and government data, please share your story.

If you're working as a supplier to a public sector or government organisation, we'd really like to hear about your work. Ideally, sessions will be run in partnership, focusing on what you've achieved together rather than the capabilities of specific software or companies.

What’s in it for you

Run your own session or event as a host, joining DWP Digital, Central Digital and Data Office, Office for National Statistics, Open Data Institute and many others who have already signed up to run sessions.

You’re welcome to contact the organisers at dataconnect21@digital.cabinet-office.gov.uk to talk through your ideas.

If you host a session, DataConnect21 will connect you with people from across the public sector, central and local government – product owners, service delivery, operational and policy people as well as those from the digital, data and technology and analysis functions.

You’ll be in good company. The following are some of the organisations that will be taking part:

  • DWP Digital
  • HM Revenue and Customs (HMRC)
  • Department for Business, Energy and Industrial Strategy (BEIS)
  • Department for International Trade (DIT)
  • NHS Digital
  • Ordnance Survey

The benefits to organisations of engaging with DataConnect21

Joining us at this event will help your organisation:
  • showcase successes and promote collaboration around the opportunities that come from better use of data across the public sector, connecting this with direct benefits to citizens
  • connect data and digital policy and technical teams across government, local government and the public sector
  • incentivise better practice and help build data capability across government and support delivery of the National Data Strategy missions
  • use learnings from the event to inform policy development and increase collaboration between data policy and practice specialists across government

Next steps for speakers

Tell us the topic of your session and what day and time you’d like to run it. Email us at dataconnect21@digital.cabinet-office.gov.uk as soon as you can.

The DataConnect21 organising team will get back to you within 3 working days to confirm we’ve received your session pledge.

Then you’ll need to:

  • plan the session - if you are running the session on your own, we can help if you email us
  • use online meeting tools to run your session - we’ll send you information on accessibility and privacy as part of our support to you
  • decide whether your session will be open to all or limited to a specific audience

The DataConnect21 organising team will provide:

  • an open schedule where you can add your session at the day and time you choose
  • communications across government about the event, bringing a wide audience to your session
  • advice and support on accessibility, technology and session planning to help you get the best out of your session
  • open office hours to get advice, connect with other session hosts or test your setup

Decide whether your session will be open or closed

This is something that you can choose, based on the content of your session. Please note that DataConnect21 will have a wide public sector and possibly private sector audience.

Some people choose to share an open link, for example when using webinar software. Others chose to ask participants to email or register for the link so they can see who’s attending. Either way is fine and up to you.

Ideas for session formats

Show and tells

Show and tells are a great way to engage people with a product or service you're developing, or a new technique or platform you’ve found useful.

You can connect with DataConnect21 in the following ways:

  • arrange a show and tell for a cross-government audience as part of our planned schedule for DataConnect21 week - you bring the topic, we’ll bring the audience
  • use the DataConnect21 backgrounds or logo with your planned show and tell talk

Community meetups

Meetups are a great way to discuss the opportunities and challenges with data across government.

You can connect with DataConnect21 in the following ways:

  • sign up for our mailing list to find out about meetups taking place during DataConnect21
  • arrange a meetup of networks you're a part of during DataConnect21 week and let us know
  • organise a discussion for a cross-government audience as part of our planned schedule for DataConnect21 week - you set the topic, we’ll bring the people

Share a skill

Do you know some really useful data shortcuts using Python?
Are you a developer who has set up an API instance in super-quick time?
Can you explain why a data model is useful to a non-technical audience?

Share your skills and knowledge with DataConnect21 by running a short workshop or skillshare session during DataConnect21 week, using our backgrounds and logo. You set up the session, we’ll bring the participants.

Present a case study or data story

Case studies and storytelling are a great way to share learning across government.

You can connect with DataConnect21 in the following ways:

  • contact us to find out about case study and storytelling sessions taking place during DataConnect21
  • arrange to tell the story of your team’s work to the rest of your organisation
  • organise a case study session as part of our open schedule for DataConnect21 week - you set the topic, we’ll bring the people

Write or record a post and share it with us

If you enjoy following data people on social media, why not take the leap and write, record or share your own data story on the social media platform of your choice during DataConnect21?

Writing about what you’ve learnt or your work around data is a great way to reflect on it and share your learnings with others.

If you share your story with us, we’ll help promote it via social media and might even invite you to share it as a CDDO blog post.

You can connect with DataConnect21 on social media using the tag #DataConnect21 by:

  • sharing your story on LinkedIn, Instagram and Twitter or other social media platform
  • liking and repost other people’s stories
  • if you’re in a network of data people, encourage others to share their story or create a joint story and share on your favourite social platform
