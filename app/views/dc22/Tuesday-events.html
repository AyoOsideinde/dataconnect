10:00am to 11:00am AD|ARC: Bringing together data to understand farms and farming households

AD|ARC – Administrative Data | Agricultural Research Collection – is an ambitious and innovative collaboration between ADR UK, academics, and government bodies, to bring together a wide-range of data about farmers, farming households, and farm business into the first UK-wide, agriculturally-focussed data platform. When constructed, the research-ready data collection will provide an invaluable resource to enable research, which will help to inform policy debate and decision making, helping to improve prosperity, resilience, and well-being in this sector.

The data collection brings together information about individuals working and living on farms – including census, education, and health data – alongside information about farm businesses – including data about farming activities, income, and geographical information. In order to create such a comprehensive data collection for all of the UK, we have been working across government departments and agencies to make sure we have comprehensive and comparable coverage of these areas. Alongside this we are working closely with data owners, processors, and researchers to make sure that the data being produced by this process across the four nations meets expectations.

Whilst the collection will enable research in a number of important areas of policy, our presentation focuses on the work before this – how we have gone about bringing these data together, and how we have worked across different national data and policy landscapes, as we discuss some of the challenges, successes and opportunities that the project has encountered. As well as offering information about the data itself, we hope that the presentation can provide valuable insight into this kind of pan-UK working, with discussion of lessons learned and ways of working developed.

Format: Presentation
Session leads: Jen Hampton (Office for National Statistics) & Nicholas Webster (ADR Wales, Wales Government)
Open to: All

11:00am to 11:30am Geospatial data standards: what are they and why do they matter?

Hear from Ordnance Survey’s geospatial data standards lead Allan Jamieson on what standards are, what they do, how they work and why we should all be spending a little bit more time getting to know why they matter.

Geospatial data standards are much more than just technical documents to implement, although they do perform that important function, they are the glue that helps to integrate all types of geospatial data.

Standards are key to making geospatial data Findable Accessible Interoperable and Reusable (FAIR), and they also represent our willingness to collaborate internationally, improving the consistency in the delivery of geospatial data and services, improving outcomes and enhancing value for money.

This session will include an introduction to the recently launched National Geospatial Data Standards Register, a collaboration between the Geospatial Commission (Cabinet Office), the BSI standards committee on geographic information, and Ordnance Survey.

This session is suitable for all professionals and practitioners working with, or wanting to work with geographic information. There are no restrictions – all are welcome, regardless of sector.

Format: Presentation
Session lead: Allan Jamieson, Data Standards Lead, Ordnance Survey

11:00am to 12:00pm Data First: Harnessing the potential of linked administrative data for the justice system

Data First is a pioneering data-linking programme led by the Ministry of Justice and funded by ADR UK (Administrative Data Research UK). The programme links administrative datasets from across the justice system and with other government departments to provide powerful new insights on justice system users, their pathways, and outcomes across a range of public services.

The session will provide an overview of the Data First programme, the achievements to date and the research potential of the Data First datasets. We will demonstrate how linked administrative data available through the ground-breaking Data First programme can be effectively used for research, how we have built relationships with partners and how the programme is providing a rich resource to develop the evidence base for government policy and practice. We will share lessons learnt from the programme so far to encourage and promote the potential and benefits of the routine sharing of administrative data for academic research.

Additionally, we will present key findings from descriptive analyses conducted by the Ministry of Justice using our linked criminal courts data and findings from a Ministry of Justice and Department for Education publication on the education and social care background of children who had been cautioned or sentenced for an offence.

Overall, the session will demonstrate how linked administrative data available through the pioneering Data First programme can be effectively used for research for policy impact.

Format: Presentation
Session leads: Georgina Eaton, Kieran Compton, Kirby King and Kylie Hill
Open to: All

4:00pm to 4:50pm GovNER: Enriching GOV.UK Metadata with Natural Language Processing

Government organisations can benefit from having more structured, machine-readable metadata about their data estates. Only by systematically gathering and managing data about their data (that is, metadata), can they confidently aspire to be data driven institutions.

As the Data Products team in the Government Digital Service, we have been striving to enrich the content on GOV.UK with semantic metadata to improve the machine readability of the site. This will act as an enabler for more advanced analytical techniques and applications, like improved information findability, content auditing, question answering and content recommendations, which will ultimately benefit internal colleagues and GOV.UK end-users as a result.

In this session, we will discuss how we have leveraged a Natural Language Processing (NLP) technique known as Named Entity Recognition (NER) and pre-trained language models, like BERT and RoBERTa, to extract essential metadata from the site. We will share the journey of the project, including experiences of building a business case, creating training data and schema, fine-tuning deep learning models, agile collaboration and development, challenges and lessons learned, and ideas for next steps.

We hope this will spark a conversation with teams in other organisations who are working on similar projects.

Format: Presentation
Session leads: Alessia Tosi, Rory Hurley, Mohamed Abdisalam
Open to: All
Signup link: Eventbrite page
