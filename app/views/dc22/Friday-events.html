10.30am to 11.15am Interoperability One Data

The Interoperability programme is enabling systems and services which span departmental boundaries to connect data together seamlessly to modernise our ways of working. These projects will improve data flows between departments, open up shared access to technology and workspaces and support a more coherent approach to understanding and building capability across the Civil Service.

We will present overviews of four key initiatives with a focus on skills taxonomies, traceability of data and automation, APIs and data standards.

Attendees will have the opportunity to comment and question project leads on what the changes mean for them and their departments.

The session will be an introduction and overview of IOD by Richard Wilkes, then project show and tells for the 4 projects:
- Workforce Data: Nia Williams (focused on skills taxonomy)
- Staff Transfers: Claire Comerford (traceability of data and automation)
- Integration Hub: Mark Watson (APIs and patterns)
- CEI: Bleddyn Preece (data standards and integrity)

Format: Presentation with Q&A
Session leads: Richard Wilkes, Nia Williams, Claire Comerford, Mark Watson and Bleddyn Preece

11.00am to 11:30am Investigating the impact of UK sanctions on DIT activity: How we used Data Workspace and NLP to identify sanctioned companies

We will be providing an overview of a recent project undertaken to identify potential matches between records of companies, contacts and investment projects on our customer databases and the GOV.UK Sanctions List.

This work took place in light of the Russian invasion of Ukraine in order to coordinate DIT international activity with the sectorial financial and investment restrictions in place in the Russia sanctions regime.

This session is recommended to anyone with an interest in data science, analysis and extraction, in particular natural language progressing methods (NLP), as well as anyone with a general interest in geopolitics and international relations.

Format: Show and Tell
Session lead: Leah Levy
Open to: UK government/public sector only (tbc)
Session resources: http://dataconnect.api.gov.uk/
Signup link: Eventbrite page

12.00pm to 1.00pm The Government Data Maturity Model: Meet the Model

In order to deliver the ambitions of the National Data Strategy, we need to understand how well government organisations are approaching data and data management.

Over the past two years, the Government Data Quality Hub has been developing and testing a maturity model that will help organisations to assess themselves, then identify changes that will help them improve their data maturity. This model will be taken forward by the Central Digital & Data Office in Cabinet Office, and rolled out across government.

This session will take you on a tour of the model, looking at its structure and how different aspects of data maturity are reflected. There won’t be time to look at everything but it will give you a n overview and a better idea of how it works in practice.

This is one of four sessions across the week that feature the Data Maturity Model.

Format: Presentation with time for questions
Session lead: Matt Thompson, Government Data Quality Hub
Open to: All
