2:00pm to 3:00pm - An introduction to the Data Standards Authority

The Data Standards Authority (DSA) was set up to make it easier and more effective to share and use data across government.

It’s a cross government authority, part of the Central Digital and Data Office (CDDO) that’s putting in place a common core infrastructure to join up government and fix the foundations with which it operates, as part of the National Data Strategy.


In this session, the DSA team, including policy lead Firoze Salim, will talk more about the purpose and current work of the DSA, the importance of data standards in ensuring a trusted, joined-up and responsive government and how you can get involved with our work.

3:00pm to 4:00pm Near real time use of publicly available Environment Agency data

The Environment Agency is classed as a First responder and as such it is vital that the data they get is as near real time as is possible, especially when citizens’ lives and livelihoods depend on it.
