9:30am to 10:30am DataConnect21: Do we need a cross-Government Data Management Community?

The Government Data Quality Hub and the Data Standards Authority created DataConnect21 to give people from across government and the public sector working with data an opportunity to come together to share their challenges and successes. But where do we go from here?

One of the opportunities in the National Data Strategy is a new cross-government Data Management Community.

We know there are many people working on data management across government and the public sector. And there are many different data communities. We’re interested in finding out if these communities are providing you with the support you need? Are they embedding silos across government or breaking them down? Are there gaps? What even IS data management anyway?

This session is open to anyone who is working on data management, even if it’s not your ‘official’ job title.

We’ll be looking at the following question:

  • What’s the challenge with bringing people together around data management, and is a cross-government community a good solution?

Additional questions:

  • How would a new cross-gov data management community add value to your work?
  • How would a cross-gov data management community collaborate with existing data communities and networks inside and outside of government?
  • How do we make sure the people who need it most can be a part of it?

10:00am to 11:00am Using data standards to help vulnerable people: Improving public services for those need them most

People who need assistance from social, health or other public services have a huge variety of needs and accessibility requirements. Services like mental health support, housing advice, digital inclusion and social wellbeing need to be easy for users to find and interact with. They need to get the right information as quickly and efficiently as possible. Unfortunately this isn’t always the case.

In this session, you’ll hear from the Data Standards Authority team who are working on introducing a government data standard for exchanging and publishing information on local public services: https://dataingovernment.blog.gov.uk/2021/04/28/vulnerable-people-services-data-standard/
The session will include experts from Essex County Council, Porism, Placecube, the Local Government Association, and will focus on the story so far in implementing the Open Referral UK standard across government .

2.30pm to 3.30pm: The Border Flow Service: Safe, Secure and Centralised Data Sharing for UK Border Readiness

Since December 2020 the Border Flow Service has been providing a critical xHMG capability that has increased the resilience of the border by powering analysis on flow of goods at the border, commodity composition, empty freight flow, land-bridge usage, predicting passenger volumes in response to international travel restrictions easing, and to plan resources around border health measures.
Drawing from over 32 HMG, commercial and open sources, the data and analytical capability delivered by the Border Flow Service is used to respond to emerging issues, in real-time, at the border including emergencies and changing trends, with agility.
This will be an opportunity to share the X-HMG use case for such a service, the data sharing challenges the team overcame, the technology underpinning it and future evolutions of the service.

Format: Show and Tell

Audience: This session would be of interest to data analysts and strategic management who are looking to ensure data quality, consistency and practical application of good data governance principles.
