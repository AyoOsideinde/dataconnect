9:30am to 10:30am Government Data Management Community

The Government Data Quality Hub and the Data Standards Authority created DataConnect to give people from across government and the public sector working with data an opportunity to come together to share their challenges and successes. But where do we go from here?

One of the opportunities in the National Data Strategy is a new cross-government Data Management Community.

We know there are many people working on data management across government and the public sector. And there are many different data communities. We’re interested in finding out if these communities are providing you with the support you need? Are they embedding silos across government or breaking them down? Are there gaps? What even IS data management anyway?

This session is open to anyone who is working on data management, even if it’s not your ‘official’ job title.

We’ll be looking at the following question:

  • What’s the challenge with bringing people together around data management, and is a cross-government community a good solution?

Additional questions:

  • How would a new cross-gov data management community add value to your work?
  • How would a cross-gov data management community collaborate with existing data communities and networks inside and outside of government?
  • How do we make sure the people who need it most can be a part of it?

Format: Discussion
Speaker: Rebecca Strickland, Government Data Quality Hub & Sarah Johns Data Standards Authority

Length of session: 60 minutes

2:00pm to 3:00pm Boosting data science capability through collaborations outside the public sector (ONS)

The ONS Data Science Campus Capability Team are carrying out a review of our collaborations with Academic Institutions, Commercial Organisations, Charities and NSI’s (amongst others). As part of this review, we would like to speak with colleagues across public sector organisations to determine:


  • what collaborations they have had/have with these kinds of organisations (with a focus on using collaborations to increase data science capability)
  • the successes and /or failures of these collaborations
  • whether they would be interested in utilising our collaborative agreements to increase data science capability within their organisations
  • how collaborative agreements can be used to increase data science capability across the public sector

We would also like to hear from organisations outside of the public sector with regards to their experiences of collaborating to increase data science capability and are also keen to hear from organisations that are interested in collaborating with us.
