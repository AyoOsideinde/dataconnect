1.00pm to 2.00pm: Building DDaT capability and skills across the UK

The Declaration on Government Reform policy paper, published in June, outlines how the Civil Service and Ministers will reform government together to deliver better for citizens.

It states, “We will look beyond London to all corners of the UK, as part of our mission to be a government more like the country we serve. More civil servants, including senior leaders, will work outside of the capital, joining the many dedicated front line staff already based in towns and cities across the UK.”

Therefore, a key action for 2021 is to implement plans to move 22,000 roles out of London by 2030, including 50% of Senior Civil Servant (SCS) roles, confirming at least five major departmental relocations this year.

As part of the GDS and Central Digital and Data Office location strategy, many new roles will be created in Manchester and Bristol, across a range of professions and capabilities, for the next decade and beyond.

This session from Manchester Digital will discuss the current digital and data skills picture in Manchester and the UK, the challenges employers can overcome, and the initiatives that can be used to recruit and retain UK wide digital, data and technology specialists.

3:30pm to 4:00pm The Supporting Families Programme - using data to support vulnerable families

Description: A talk about the impact and importance that data has played within the Supporting Families Programme
Rebecca Leader is the Data Transformation Lead for the Supporting Families Programme in the Department for Levelling Up, Housing and Communities. Her role is focused on improving the use of data, systems and analytics in local authority partnerships to help support families through the identification and prevention of multiple and complex issues. She has worked to develop tools to provide a framework for data maturity, allow local authorities to assess their own maturity in this field and monitor national progress. Her approach is to equip local authorities to build solid foundations and systems on which robust analytics can be applied. Rebecca has worked in a police force on research and strategy planning, led data teams in a local authority across Supporting Families and Early Help, Youth Offending, Childrens Services.
