3:00pm to 4:00pm Transparency in Public Procurement: the need for a standard for beneficial ownership data

Transparency around beneficial owners has been a topic of global interest for a long time with a growing number of governments making formal commitments to collecting beneficial ownership data. Most recently, on 1 September 2021, the UK government published a [Declaration of National Commitment](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/beneficial-ownership-disclosure-principles/declaration-of-national-commitment-to-meet-the-beneficial-ownership-transparency-disclosure-principles-accessible-version) to meet the beneficial ownership transparency disclosure principles.

In this session, you’ll hear from the Data Standards Authority (part of the Central Digital and Data Office) who together with the Global Digital Marketplace (part of Government Digital Service) launched [a consultation](https://dataingovernment.blog.gov.uk/2021/04/08/who-really-benefits-from-uk-business-ownership/) last April to endorse the Beneficial Ownership Data Standard. They will provide an overview of current work supporting government's objectives in the wider context of Business Identity.

You’ll also hear from Open Ownership who have developed the [Beneficial Ownership Data Standard (BODS)](http://standard.openownership.org/en/0.2.0/primer/whatisthebods.html), whilst Isabella Fudge (Policy Advisor, Open Government at Cabinet Office) will talk about the UK government's commitments to transparency in the context of the [National Action Plan](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/uk-national-action-plan-for-open-government-2019-2021/uk-national-action-plan-for-open-government-2019-2021).
