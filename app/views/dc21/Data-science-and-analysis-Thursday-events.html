2:30pm to 3:00pm The utility of data in government: the case of cycling

The Treasury has been running a few data insight experimentations over the course of the past year to drive better policy making. We have tracked value for money decisions and supporting evaluations by mapping geospatial data at a granular level – a relatively nascent endeavour within the Organisation – and have relied heavily on usually disparate data sources of varying quality which have been captured by Local Authorities of differing capacity and by private sector organisations.

We will present a case study from our cycling data insights project, which aimed to develop our understanding of the current data landscape and demonstrate the utility of data in government. As part of this project, we developed an experimental, proof-of-concept dashboard as a means of visualising the extent of cycling data maturity in Local Authorities, so as to enhance our understanding of cycling investment and its impact.

This session is for the benefit of government colleagues and will include a showcase of the dashboard and a discussion of how we used data when assembling our visualisations.

Speakers: Bandhu Das (HMT) & Jerome Greenfield (HMT)
