RAP: Software engineering in analysis!

Reproducible Analytical Pipelines (RAP) are the application of good practices from software engineering in analysis and research. They make analysis and research more reproducible, better quality, more trustworthy, and efficient. The recent Office for Stats Regulation review of RAP says "there is a huge potential to apply RAP principles both in and beyond official statistics".


RAP is the linchpin of the digital transformation of government analysis. Through the high quality automation it brings, more effort can be directed towards building the research products that our users need. Recent successes like the UK Coronavirus Dashboard show how powerful government data can be in answering the needs of the public when sound RAP principles are applied.

However, learning the practices in RAP represent a substantial challenge for many government analysts - especially in a context where there is little prioritisation to build this capability and where the tools needed can be difficult to acquire. Our Coding in Analysis and Research survey shows that although most analysts feel it's important to implement the RAP principles in their work, many fewer are able to do so.

Our approach to RAP is multi-pronged to tackle the cultural, capability, and technical barriers that exist.



We operate a Champions network that engages across government and delivers mentoring, training, and advocacy within their respective departments,
We deliver consultancy and mentorship for organisations beginning their journey,
We advocate for the digital transformation of analysis with RAP at senior levels in government,
We monitor progress towards RAP and evaluate interventions,
We role model good practice in our own analytical work.

Through our research of coding in analysis across government and the research of colleagues we identify next steps for public sector organisations and advocate for the inclusion of RAP principles at the heart of government analytical strategy.

3:00pm to 4:00pm Data Literacy – What is it and what do I do with it?

Organisations are constantly looking for the competitive edge to remain successful, profitable and, ultimately, long-lasting. Regardless of whether you work with a multi-national organisation or a government department, data is central to that success. What is becoming apparent is that the vast majority of employees within organisations don’t have the confidence with data to truly unlock its potential. In fact, research shows that only 1 in 4 business decision makers would categorise themselves as being fully data literate. At executive level, this number is about 1 in 3. And, crucially, organisations don’t know what steps to take to rectify the skills gap – to place data literacy at the heart of their business.

Join this session with Chris Hansford from the Environment Agency for a Q&A on this subject. All welcome.
